What Do Percentiles Mean?

A percentile tells you the distance you can reliably count on. If your 7-iron carry is P75 = 155 yards, that means 75% of your shots with that club carried at least 155 yards — it's your "dependable" number for course management.

Lower percentiles (P25, P50) represent your most conservative estimates — useful when there's trouble short of the target. Higher percentiles (P90, P95) reflect your best-case distances — useful when you need to clear a hazard or reach a par 5 in two.

Example: With P75 selected and a 9-iron carry of 130 yards, you know that 3 out of 4 shots will fly at least 130. That's a great number for picking clubs on approach shots.
